## What Makes Dallas Special

Dallas is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Big D Energy," this north texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Dallas is big, bold, and unapologetically Texan with world-class arts, legendary BBQ, and the iconic Reunion Tower skyline.

## Things to Do in Dallas

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Dallas has something for everyone.

- **Visit the Sixth Floor Museum at Dealey Plaza** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Dallas on the map.
- **Explore the Dallas Arts District** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Eat BBQ in Deep Ellum** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Dallas

Texas cuisine shines in Dallas. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $50-$150 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Dallas

Accommodations range from budget options ($50/night) to mid-range hotels ($150/night) to luxury properties ($400/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Dallas

The best months to visit are March through November when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Dallas

Fly into Dallas/Fort Worth International Airport (DFW) or Dallas Love Field (DAL). Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
