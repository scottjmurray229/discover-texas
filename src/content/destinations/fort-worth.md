## What Makes Fort Worth Special

Fort Worth is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Where the West Begins," this north texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Fort Worth is the real-deal cowtown with the Stockyards, world-class museums, and a western culture uniquely Texan.

## Things to Do in Fort Worth

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Fort Worth has something for everyone.

- **Watch the cattle drive at the Stockyards** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Fort Worth on the map.
- **Visit the Kimbell Art Museum** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Explore Sundance Square** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Fort Worth

Texas cuisine shines in Fort Worth. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $45-$130 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Fort Worth

Accommodations range from budget options ($45/night) to mid-range hotels ($130/night) to luxury properties ($350/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Fort Worth

The best months to visit are March through November when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Fort Worth

Fly into DFW Airport or drive from Dallas (35 min) via I-30. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
