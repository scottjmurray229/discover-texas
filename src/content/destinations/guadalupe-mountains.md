## What Makes Guadalupe Mountains Special

Guadalupe Mountains is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Highest Point in Texas," this west texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Guadalupe Mountains National Park holds the highest point in Texas at 8,751 feet with ancient reef fossils.

## Things to Do in Guadalupe Mountains

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Guadalupe Mountains has something for everyone.

- **Summit Guadalupe Peak (highest in TX)** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Guadalupe Mountains on the map.
- **Hike McKittrick Canyon in fall colors** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Explore the salt flats** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Guadalupe Mountains

Texas cuisine shines in Guadalupe Mountains. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $25-$80 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Guadalupe Mountains

Accommodations range from budget options ($25/night) to mid-range hotels ($80/night) to luxury properties ($180/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Guadalupe Mountains

The best months to visit are October through April when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Guadalupe Mountains

Drive from El Paso (110 min) via US-62/180. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
