## What Makes Eagle Pass Special

Eagle Pass is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Fort Duncan and Border Heritage," this south texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Eagle Pass is a historic border crossing with Fort Duncan and the Kickapoo Lucky Eagle Casino.

## Things to Do in Eagle Pass

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Eagle Pass has something for everyone.

- **Visit historic Fort Duncan** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Eagle Pass on the map.
- **Try the Kickapoo Lucky Eagle Casino** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Cross to Piedras Negras** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Eagle Pass

Texas cuisine shines in Eagle Pass. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $25-$70 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Eagle Pass

Accommodations range from budget options ($25/night) to mid-range hotels ($70/night) to luxury properties ($160/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Eagle Pass

The best months to visit are October through April when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Eagle Pass

Drive from San Antonio (2.5 hours) via US-57. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
