## What Makes El Paso Special

El Paso is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Sun City on the Border," this west texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

El Paso is Texas western outpost with stunning Franklin Mountains, vibrant border culture, and the best Mexican food.

## Things to Do in El Paso

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, El Paso has something for everyone.

- **Hike the Franklin Mountains** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts El Paso on the map.
- **Eat authentic Mexican food in the Mission District** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Explore El Paso Mission Trail** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in El Paso

Texas cuisine shines in El Paso. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $40-$110 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in El Paso

Accommodations range from budget options ($40/night) to mid-range hotels ($110/night) to luxury properties ($250/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit El Paso

The best months to visit are October through April when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to El Paso

Fly into El Paso International Airport (ELP) on the far western tip of Texas. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
