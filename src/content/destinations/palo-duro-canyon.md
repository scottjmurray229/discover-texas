## What Makes Palo Duro Canyon Special

Palo Duro Canyon is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Grand Canyon of Texas," this panhandle gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Palo Duro Canyon is the second-largest canyon in the US at 120 miles long with stunning red rock formations.

## Things to Do in Palo Duro Canyon

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Palo Duro Canyon has something for everyone.

- **Hike the Lighthouse Trail** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Palo Duro Canyon on the map.
- **See the outdoor musical TEXAS** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Mountain bike the canyon trails** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Palo Duro Canyon

Texas cuisine shines in Palo Duro Canyon. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $25-$80 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Palo Duro Canyon

Accommodations range from budget options ($25/night) to mid-range hotels ($80/night) to luxury properties ($180/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Palo Duro Canyon

The best months to visit are April through October when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Palo Duro Canyon

Drive from Amarillo (25 min) via TX-217. Fly into Amarillo (AMA). Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
