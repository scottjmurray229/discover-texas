Fredericksburg is the Texas Hill Country's most popular small town — a German immigrant community founded in 1846 that's now the center of the Texas wine industry, with 60+ wineries along US-290 (the 'Wine Road'), the exceptional National Museum of the Pacific War (Admiral Chester Nimitz's birthplace), and peach orchards producing the best peaches in Texas each June through August.

Fredericksburg is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Wine Country and German Heritage," this hill country gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Fredericksburg is the heart of Texas Wine Country with German heritage, 50+ wineries, and stunning Enchanted Rock.

## Things to Do in Fredericksburg

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Fredericksburg has something for everyone.

- **Tour the Texas Wine Trail** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Fredericksburg on the map.
- **Hike Enchanted Rock** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Explore Main Street shops** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Fredericksburg

Texas cuisine shines in Fredericksburg. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $50-$160 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Fredericksburg

Accommodations range from budget options ($50/night) to mid-range hotels ($160/night) to luxury properties ($400/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Fredericksburg

The best months to visit are March through November when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Fredericksburg

Drive from Austin (80 min) or San Antonio (70 min) via US-290 or I-10. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
