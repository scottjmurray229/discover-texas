## What Makes Alpine Special

Alpine is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Gateway to West Texas," this west texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Alpine is the hub of the Big Bend region with a friendly college town, great food, and easy access to far west Texas.

## Things to Do in Alpine

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Alpine has something for everyone.

- **Explore the Museum of the Big Bend** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Alpine on the map.
- **Catch a show at Railroad Blues** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Drive the scenic Hwy 118 south** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Alpine

Texas cuisine shines in Alpine. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $30-$90 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Alpine

Accommodations range from budget options ($30/night) to mid-range hotels ($90/night) to luxury properties ($220/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Alpine

The best months to visit are October through April when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Alpine

Drive from Midland-Odessa (2.5 hours). Amtrak Sunset Limited stops here. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
