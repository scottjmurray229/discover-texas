## What Makes Kerrville Special

Kerrville is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Heart of the Hills," this hill country gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Kerrville sits along the Guadalupe River and is a haven for artists, kayakers, and Hill Country lovers.

## Things to Do in Kerrville

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Kerrville has something for everyone.

- **Kayak the Guadalupe River** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Kerrville on the map.
- **Visit the Museum of Western Art** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Attend the Kerrville Folk Festival** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Kerrville

Texas cuisine shines in Kerrville. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $35-$100 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Kerrville

Accommodations range from budget options ($35/night) to mid-range hotels ($100/night) to luxury properties ($240/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Kerrville

The best months to visit are March through October when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Kerrville

Drive from San Antonio (65 min) via I-10. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
