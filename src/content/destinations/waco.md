## What Makes Waco Special

Waco is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Heart of Texas Heritage," this central texas gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Waco sits at the crossroads of Texas with Magnolia Market, Dr Pepper, and a growing food scene along the Brazos River.

## Things to Do in Waco

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Waco has something for everyone.

- **Shop at Magnolia Market at the Silos** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Waco on the map.
- **Tour the Dr Pepper Museum** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Explore Cameron Park trails** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Waco

Texas cuisine shines in Waco. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $40-$110 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Waco

Accommodations range from budget options ($40/night) to mid-range hotels ($110/night) to luxury properties ($250/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Waco

The best months to visit are March through November when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Waco

Drive from Dallas (90 min) or Austin (90 min) via I-35. Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
