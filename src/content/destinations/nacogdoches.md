## What Makes Nacogdoches Special

Nacogdoches is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Oldest Town in Texas," this piney woods gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Nacogdoches claims the title of Texas oldest town with deep Piney Woods charm and brick streets.

## Things to Do in Nacogdoches

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Nacogdoches has something for everyone.

- **Walk the historic brick streets** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Nacogdoches on the map.
- **Visit the Old Stone Fort Museum** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Hike the Lanana Creek Trail** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Nacogdoches

Texas cuisine shines in Nacogdoches. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $30-$85 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Nacogdoches

Accommodations range from budget options ($30/night) to mid-range hotels ($85/night) to luxury properties ($180/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Nacogdoches

The best months to visit are March through November when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Nacogdoches

Drive from Houston (2.5 hours) via US-59 or from Dallas (3 hours). Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
