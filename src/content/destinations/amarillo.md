## What Makes Amarillo Special

Amarillo is one of Texas's most beloved destinations. Known as the "Gateway to Palo Duro Canyon," this panhandle gem delivers authentic Lone Star experiences you won't find anywhere else.

Amarillo is the gateway to the Panhandle with Palo Duro Canyon, Route 66 nostalgia, and the Big Texan steak challenge.

## Things to Do in Amarillo

Whether you're looking for outdoor adventure, cultural experiences, or great food, Amarillo has something for everyone.

- **Explore Palo Duro Canyon** — A must-do for any visitor. This is the experience that puts Amarillo on the map.
- **Drive the Route 66 Historic District** — Another highlight that showcases the best of the area.
- **Take the Big Texan steak challenge** — Round out your visit with this local favorite.

## Where to Eat in Amarillo

Texas cuisine shines in Amarillo. From legendary BBQ joints to Tex-Mex favorites and local specialties, you'll eat well here. Budget about $35-$100 per person per day for food.

## Where to Stay in Amarillo

Accommodations range from budget options ($35/night) to mid-range hotels ($100/night) to luxury properties ($220/night). Book ahead during peak season and major events.

## Best Time to Visit Amarillo

The best months to visit are April through October when temperatures are comfortable and outdoor activities are at their best. Summer months can be extremely hot across most of Texas.

## Getting to Amarillo

Fly into Rick Husband Amarillo International Airport (AMA). Car rental is the most practical way to explore the area.
